I'm a linguist by training and a data person by practice. My path has taken me through teaching, multilingual coordination, content analysis, and procurement — each role a different lens on the same underlying question: how language carries meaning across people, systems, and contexts.

Over time, I noticed that the questions I found most interesting — how language works, how patterns emerge across texts, and how the trends shift through time  — were the same questions that computational tools were getting better at answering. That realization led me to pursue a Master's in Linguistic Diversity & Digital Humanities at the University of Helsinki, where I worked across corpus linguistics, syntactic parsing, machine translation, and digital humanities methods including network analysis and OCR while also expanding my theoretical grounding in frameworks like Construction Grammar.

Most recently, this has taken the form of Lanna LLM, an ongoing project evaluating and improving LLMs on Northern Thai Dialect — a question that sits squarely at the intersection of my linguistic roots and computational interests.
